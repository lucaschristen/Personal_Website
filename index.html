About

I am a PhD student in the Komurasaki & Koizumi Laboratory at the University of Tokyo (enrolled October 2024). My work explores high‑temperature laser processing of lunar regolith, with an eye toward in‑situ resource utilization (ISRU) for future lunar industry and exploration.

ISRU Laser Processing High‑temperature Diagnostics Lunar Regolith

Research

Current interests include vaporization behavior of lunar simulants, multi‑wavelength pyrometry, and chemically selective oxide processing under controlled atmospheres.

Laser-Driven Processing of Lunar Regolith

Studying mass-loss, vapor composition, and melt evolution under high-flux CW fiber lasers.

Methods: OES, ratio/MWP thermometry, high-speed imaging

Temperature Diagnostics

Building a robust pipeline for multi-wavelength pyrometry with calibration routines and uncertainty analysis.

Tools: Python, optics/photometry, calibration lamps, integrating spheres
